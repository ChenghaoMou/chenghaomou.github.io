AdEMAMix

AdamW uses two hyperparameters β1​ and β2​ to control how much influence the old gradients have over the current the step — large values emphasis more on old values and thus more insensitive to current gradients and small values allow the models to update to current batch more quickly but forget old updates more quickly. This observation prompts a new modification of AdamW: The AdEMAMix Optimizer: Better, Faster, Older:

with a new addition of m2​, allowing β3​ to learn more from old updates while keeping the original hyperparameters β1​,β2​ the same. However, this also necessitates a new scheduler for β3​ and α:

α(t)β3(t)​​=min(Tα​t​α,α)=min(exp((1−Tβ3​​t​)lnβ3​+Tβ3​​t​lnβstart​lnβstart​lnβ3​​),β3​)​​

β3​=0.9999,α∈[4,10],βstart​=β1​ and Tα​=Tβ3​​=T works well in practice.

Based on the experiments, AdEMAMix shows a more smooth and faster convergence and retains data influence/knowledge better during the training.
